Essential Cybersecurity Practices

Building Your First Line of Defense: Strong Passwords

A strong password is the first and most crucial step in protecting your online accounts. It should be unique, complex, and difficult to guess.

  • Use a combination of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.
  • Aim for at least 12 characters.
  • Avoid using personal information or common words.
  • Consider using a passphrase (a series of words) that's easy for you to remember but hard for others to guess.
  • Don't reuse passwords across multiple accounts.

Don't Get Hooked: Recognizing Phishing Scams

Phishing is a cyberattack where attackers try to trick you into giving them your personal information (like passwords or credit card details) by posing as a trustworthy entity.

  • Look for suspicious email addresses or links.
  • Be wary of urgent or threatening language.
  • Check for grammatical errors or typos.
  • Hover over links to see the actual destination before clicking.
  • Never click on links or attachments from unknown senders.

Taking Control: Managing Your Online Privacy

Social media platforms and other websites collect data about you. It's important to understand your privacy settings and adjust them to control what information is shared.

  • Review the privacy policies of the websites and apps you use.
  • Limit the personal information you share publicly.
  • Be mindful of the photos and videos you post.
  • Adjust your privacy settings to control who can see your content and contact you.

Stay Up-to-Date: The Importance of Software Updates

Software updates often include security patches that fix vulnerabilities that attackers could exploit. Keeping your software updated is crucial for preventing malware infections and other cyber threats.

  • Enable automatic updates whenever possible.
  • Regularly check for updates for your operating system, web browser, and other applications.
  • Don't ignore update notifications.

Wi-Fi Safety: Protecting Yourself on Public Networks

Public Wi-Fi networks are often unsecured, making your data vulnerable to interception by attackers.

  • Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities like online banking or shopping.
  • Use a Virtual Private Network (VPN) to encrypt your internet traffic when using public Wi-Fi.
  • Look for HTTPS in website addresses to ensure a secure connection.
